Before any other hobby or personal quality, I love problem solving. Ever since I saw the movie The Social Network, I've always wondered "How hard can this be?". Throughout my college years, I would take computer science courses, and I always enjoyed them, sometimes focusing more on the c++ course more than my required coursework. From that day forward, I knew I wanted to be an engineer! As I pushed myself through learning Ruby, Rails, and React/Redux, I began to realize that what was popping up on my screen wasn't witchcraft, but it was MAGIC. Magic of my own doing. I was latching on to any and every type of idea and concept I can start a project on, from moving "Hello World" to the opposite side of the page, to creating a Data Visualization of the hottest songs in the United States.

This passion carried me through my time at the App Academy. I was able to pair program with others who shared the same fire and desire to design and build applications and websites. I learned a lot more about the intricacies of JavaScript, the magic that is Ruby on Rails, and, the effectiveness of the D3 library visualizing data with html and styling its SVG properties with CSS.

Now in the industry, the passion has yet to fade. I still continue to learn so much more, and yet, I still have more to learn. While I'm currently teaching building web applications in Python, I'm noticing there are many more problems to solve, and even more to build.
